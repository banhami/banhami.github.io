Projects

Allen School Socialspace

CSE 441/DES 372

Worked alongside School of Design students to develop a physical interactive installation aimed at fostering a sense of community between two buildings through shared discussion prompts and response word clouds. Captured imagery and audio and consequently utilized various machine learning APIs/libraries to extract relevant data (Python, cv2, spacy, Google Speech-to-Text), created visual participant-facing projection (Processing). Participated in project showcase where building stakeholders interacted with and judged our installation – many indicated delight in the ambient nature of the installation and the digital “conversational bridge” it formed.

Experience Museums Project

HCDE 318

Worked alongside Human-Centered Design students to create a digital product to enrich and enhance the museum-going experience. Performed initial ideation (Personas, Storyboards, User-Journey Map), conducted user-research (contextual inquiry, interviews), prototyped, evaluated, and refined product design (Usability Testing, Critiques), developed and presented final product design to a panel of industry and academic professionals (HTML/CSS). Panel and academic feedback highlighted the well-researched design of our final product, visual aesthetics, and the concise yet comprehensive final portfolio site – featuring process reflections and interactive designs. Outreach

Apart from my interests in industry, I am also very passionate about broadening participation and access to computer science education. For the past two years I have been serving as an ambassador lead with the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science, working to promote diversity within the school through intentional community outreach.

Throughout my time as an ambassador, I have been able to visit over 30 schools, design and run local community conversations on topics and stereotypes surrounding tech, and talk to a countless number of students, answering their questions on what and who tech is and how they can make it a part of their lives. This Summer, I also had the opportunity to teach an introductory programming class as a part of the University of Washington's Math Science Upward Bound program.
